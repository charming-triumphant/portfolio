# Product Brief: Portfolio

## Executive Summary

Ben's portfolio website is a personal brand platform and long-term inbound engine designed to do one thing above all else: make the right hiring managers stop, engage, and reach out — and to keep working for Ben for years beyond his first job search.

It is not a CV dressed up as a website — it is a designed product that demonstrates Ben's value by its very existence. The site leads with creative design and people-first personality, then earns technical credibility through deep project case studies, reasoned stack decisions, and measurable outcomes.

Built to serve a wide NZ market — primarily Tauranga, with reach into Auckland, Hamilton, and Wellington — the site targets software developer roles first, with the CS + Marketing dual degree positioning letting the business and communication competency emerge naturally through the work itself.

Ben's core competitive advantage is the rare combination of a Bachelor of Computer Science with a second major in Marketing, real professional full stack development experience (PHP/Laravel/Vue.js in production for medical and SME clients), and a demonstrated ability to design and communicate. This is not a junior developer looking for their first break — this is a developer who already understands business, people, and product.

---

## Core Vision

### Problem Statement

Hiring managers in the NZ tech market (particularly outside Auckland) struggle to identify junior-to-mid developers who combine technical capability with business awareness and communication skills. Generic portfolios — a list of projects, a skills grid, a contact form — fail to communicate personality, design instinct, or the kind of cross-functional value that makes a hire genuinely impactful beyond writing code.

### Problem Impact

Without a differentiated portfolio:
- Ben is evaluated on the same criteria as every other junior developer
- His Marketing degree, design sensibility, and real-world professional experience are invisible
- He competes on GitHub commit counts and tutorial projects rather than on the unique value he actually brings
- Hiring managers in Tauranga have no easy way to discover him organically at all
- The long-term inbound opportunity (clients, collaborators, referral networks) is completely missed

### Why Existing Solutions Fall Short

Typical developer portfolios:
- Lead with technical skills, not personality or design ability — the wrong first impression for Ben's actual strengths
- Use static, un-maintained templates that signal lack of craft
- Have no SEO strategy — invisible to organic search in specific regional markets
- Make contact unnecessarily difficult — forms hidden in footers, no visible email, no download CV
- Don't communicate the CS + Marketing cross-discipline advantage at all
- Treat the portfolio as a job application tool rather than a multi-year business asset

### Proposed Solution

A custom-designed, high-performance portfolio website that:
1. Opens with a visually striking 3D interactive hero that immediately communicates design capability and creative confidence — leading with *emotion and personality*, not credentials
2. Progresses through a narrative scroll experience built around the through-line of **empathy + creativity** — the qualities that define Ben's unique value
3. Presents 3 deep case studies (the portfolio itself + anonymised professional work + planned carbon tracking app) with quantified outcomes, technical reasoning, and design decisions documented
4. Explicitly positions the CS + Marketing dual degree as a business-technical bridge skill — rare, valuable, named clearly — while letting the competency emerge through the work itself for developer-audience targeting
5. Makes contact and Download CV frictionless: 3 entry points, 3-field form, visible email, "I reply within 24 hours"
6. Is built and optimised to rank organically for Ben's name and relevant developer search terms in NZ regional markets (Tauranga, Auckland, Hamilton, Wellington)
7. Functions as a 3-year inbound engine — not just a job application, but a persistent professional presence that compounds over time

### Key Differentiators

1. **The CS + Marketing Bridge:** Not a pure developer. Someone who understands users, business goals, and commercial outcomes. Exceptionally rare and valuable at companies that want developers who can contribute beyond a ticket queue.

2. **Design-Led First Impression:** The site leads with creative design and personality — not a skills list. Hiring managers who care about product quality and UI craft immediately recognise a different kind of candidate. The hero is an *emotional* experience first.

3. **Already Shipping Professionally:** Real production experience building software for medical practices and SME clients (PHP/Laravel/Vue.js). Not a student project portfolio — professional work, anonymised where required.

4. **Adaptable Stack:** Currently PHP/Laravel/Vue.js professionally; actively building in C#/React to align with NZ market demand. Demonstrates market awareness and intentional skill development.

5. **The Portfolio IS the Proof:** The site itself — 3D WebGL, Lighthouse 95+, choreographed animations, frictionless contact — is documented as a case study. Every claim the site makes about Ben's abilities is simultaneously demonstrated by the site's own existence.

6. **Regional Market Presence + Inbound Strategy:** Specifically targeting Tauranga (underserved for visible developer talent) while maintaining reach into Auckland, Hamilton, and Wellington. SEO strategy targets both name-based and regional role-based search for long-term organic discovery.

7. **NZ-Relevant Portfolio Project Pipeline:** Carbon tracking app for NZ SMEs (target: Nov 2026) — a specific, real-world problem in a domain meaningful to NZ businesses. Specific beats generic every time.

#### Persona 1: The Remote-First Hiring Manager
**Representative name:** Sophie (startup CTO) or Alex (tech lead)
**Context:** Company of 10–200 people, anywhere in NZ, open to fully remote or hybrid hires. Budget for a junior-to-mid developer or a technically-capable sales/solutions person. Likely found Ben via his job application or LinkedIn — portfolio is the qualification filter before they decide whether to interview.

**Goals:**
- Quickly assess: can this person actually do the work?
- Verify cultural fit: do I want to work with this human?
- Confirm practical details: available when? Where based? Can we contact them easily?

**Pain points:**
- Reviews 20–50+ portfolios per role; most are indistinguishable
- Can't assess personality or communication ability from a GitHub profile
- Wastes time on candidates who look good on paper but can't think beyond tickets

**What makes them say YES:**
- First 30 seconds: visually impressive, immediately clear what Ben does and offers
- Case studies that show *thinking*, not just features built
- Quantified outcomes — numbers, not adjectives
- Frictionless contact or calendar booking

**Journey:**
1. Receives Ben's application → clicks portfolio link from CV or LinkedIn
2. Lands on hero → forms first impression within 5 seconds
3. Scrolls through 1–2 case studies → assesses capability and thinking style
4. Checks contact/availability → books call or sends email
5. Downloads CV → forwards internally with "check this out"

**Representative name:** David — Operations Manager or Business Owner, NZ SME (20–200 staff), sector: professional services, primary industries, healthcare, sustainability-focused businesses
**Context:** Has a software problem or digital transformation need. May not know exactly what they need built. Found Ben via word of mouth, LinkedIn, or Google search ("NZ freelance developer", "software consultant Tauranga/NZ"). Target contract types: greenfield app builds and integration work (Ben's professional strength area).

**Goals:**

- Know they won't be blinded by jargon — someone who speaks their language
- Understand what it would cost and what they'd get
- See evidence this person has solved real problems for real businesses before

- Can't evaluate technical skill directly — needs proxies for trustworthiness

**What makes them say YES:**
- Portfolio that *feels* like it was made by someone who understands business people
- Case studies that describe the **problem and outcome** in plain English, not just the tech stack
- Clear contact path, clear availability, response time promise
- The CS + Marketing combination explicitly named — signals "this developer thinks like a business person"
- Carbon tracking app case study is the hero freelance demo — specific NZ problem, full product build, relatable to sustainability-conscious NZ businesses

**Journey:**
1. Discovers Ben via Google ("developer Tauranga" / "NZ SME software developer") OR LinkedIn OR referral
2. Lands on portfolio → assesses: does this feel trustworthy and professional?
3. Reads About/case studies → looks for: has this person solved my kind of problem?
4. Sees contact section → sends inquiry or books a discovery call
5. Refers Ben to a colleague even if their own timing isn't right yet (word of mouth engine begins)

### Key UX Decisions from User Research

**Dual-audience contact section:**
- Headline: "Let's build something together" — neutral language that works for both hiring managers and freelance clients without signalling desperation or limiting positioning
- Two clearly differentiated contact paths beneath: "I'm hiring" and "I have a project" — self-segmenting, no confusion
- Both paths lead to the same low-friction 3-field form + visible email + response promise

**Same content, dual-audience writing:**
Case studies written with both technical depth AND plain-English business outcome language. The CS + Marketing positioning serves hiring managers (rare cross-discipline value) and freelance clients (trustworthy business-minded developer) simultaneously without separate content or navigation.

The portfolio is version 1.0 of Ben's future consulting agency presence. Domain consideration: `benlastname.dev` now, with potential future migration to `[agencyname].co.nz` once agency brand is established.

## Success Metrics

The portfolio's primary success metric is **inbound consulting and freelance enquiries**. Every other metric is in service of this. Employment is a passive secondary benefit — the site should work for both without optimising exclusively for either.

### User Success Metrics

**For Freelance / Consulting Clients (Primary):**
- Client lands on portfolio → understands Ben's offering within 30 seconds
- Client can initiate contact in ≤ 2 clicks from anywhere on the page
- Client receives a response within 24 hours (Ben's stated commitment)
- Client refers Ben to at least one other contact within 6 months of engagement

**For Hiring Managers (Secondary — passive benefit):**
- Hiring manager can assess capability, personality, and fit within 2 minutes
- CV downloaded and forwarded internally without friction
- Portfolio standalone strong enough to generate interview invitations without Ben needing to follow up

## MVP Scope

### Core Features (MVP — Ship This First)

The MVP is the smallest version of the portfolio that can credibly generate freelance/consulting enquiries and serve as a professional presence. It must be live, fast, indexed, and contactable.

**Recommended Tech Stack (from architecture review):**
- **Framework:** Astro — static HTML shell for all SEO-critical content, perfect Lighthouse scores by default
- **3D:** React Three Fiber as an Astro island — interactive 3D only where needed, not bundled into every page
- **Styling:** Tailwind CSS — utility-first, zero runtime overhead
- **Content structure:** CMS-ready architecture (structured content components) even without deploying a CMS — future agency evolution without rewrite
- **Hosting:** Vercel or Netlify — free tier sufficient for MVP, automatic HTTPS, global CDN

**1. Domain & Deployment**
- Custom `ben[lastname].dev` domain registered and live
- Deployed to Vercel/Netlify
- HTTPS enforced (automatic with .dev TLD)
- Google Search Console verified and sitemap submitted

**2. Hero Section — Minimalist 3D**
- Full-screen interactive 3D hero using React Three Fiber (contained, well-documented scene — impressive-but-shipped over perfect-but-never-live)
- Cursor-reactive or scroll-reactive — visually remarkable, not decorative
- Name, one-line value proposition, single CTA: "Let's build something"
- Loads in < 2 seconds despite 3D (performance non-negotiable)

**3. About / Value Proposition Section**
- CS + Marketing dual degree positioning — named explicitly
- Currently employed as full stack developer — real professional experience stated
- "Designed and built by me" callout
- Confident, evidence-led tone — no junior self-deprecation

**4. Skills Section (Confidence-Tiered)**
- Tiers: "Confident in / Actively learning / Familiar with"
- Current professional stack (PHP/Laravel/Vue.js) + learning stack (C#/React)
- One-line reasoning: "Building C#/React experience to align with NZ market demand"

**5. Projects / Case Studies — 2 minimum for MVP**
- **Case Study 1: This portfolio site** — documented as a case study (3D tech, performance score, design decisions, stack rationale)
- **Case Study 2: Anonymised professional work** — pending employer permission; honest placeholder if unavailable
- Each: problem → decisions → outcome + quantified metrics
- Stack per project with one-sentence reasoning

**6. Contact Section — Dual Path, Frictionless**
- Headline: "Let's build something together"
- Path A: "I have a project" → 3-field form (name, email, message only)
- Path B: "Download CV" → branded PDF in new tab
- Visible email address in plain text
- Response promise: "I reply within 24 hours"
- LinkedIn + GitHub icon links
- Status: "Open to consulting & freelance projects"

**7. SEO Foundation**
- Title tags + meta descriptions on all pages
- Open Graph image — branded per page
- JSON-LD schema markup (Person + SoftwareApplication per project)
- XML sitemap submitted to Google Search Console
- robots.txt configured

**8. Performance Baseline**
- Lighthouse ≥ 90: Performance, Accessibility, Best Practices, SEO
- Core Web Vitals passing
- Scores documented and displayed in portfolio-as-case-study section

**9. Console.log Easter Egg**
- Single message for DevTools visitors:
  `"Hey, you opened DevTools. That's exactly the kind of person I want to work with. → ben@[domain].dev"`

**Phase 2 — Post-launch polish:**
- Choreographed scroll animations
- Section-reactive custom cursor
- Learning velocity timeline
- "Right Now" section
- "How I work" section
- Technical footnotes on case studies
- Clean GitHub repo READMEs linked
- 404 page with personality

**Phase 3 — Later additions:**
- Carbon tracking app case study *(priority: add same day it ships, target Nov 2026)*
- Long-tail technical blog articles for SEO
- Testimonials section
- Design process artifacts in case studies
- Print-optimised CSS
- Farm IoT dashboard case study (deferred until built)

**Explicitly not in scope:**
- Blog/CMS at launch
- Dark/light mode toggle — commit to dark mode for premium feel
- Multiple language support
- Built-in booking/calendar (use external Calendly link if needed)
